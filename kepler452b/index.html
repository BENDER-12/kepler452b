What is kepler452b

Kepler-452b is an exoplanet that garnered worldwide attention as a possible "Earth cousin" when it was discovered by NASA’s Kepler space telescope in 2015. Situated about 1,400 light-years away in the constellation Cygnus, Kepler-452b orbits a star remarkably similar to our Sun, providing intriguing possibilities for scientists investigating the potential for life and the nature of distant, Earth-like worlds.

Explore Kepler-452b
  1. What So Great about Kepler?

    Kepler-452b is located in the "habitable zone" of its star, where temperatures might allow for liquid water on its surface—a key condition for life as we know it.

    he length of a year on Kepler-452b (the time it takes to orbit its star) is about 385 Earth days, which is quite similar to Earth's year of 365 days.

  2. Facts about kepler-452b

    Kepler-452b's star is older than our Sun, meaning it has more stable conditions, which could be favorable for long-term habitability. However, as its star ages and becomes hotter, it may eventually cause a greenhouse effect similar to that on Venus, making the planet less hospitable over time
